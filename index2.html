WWE experience

In early 1989, Avitia began wrestling on the independent circuit in Ontario and the Great Lakes under the name Sexton Hardcastle. Avitia formed a tag team known as Chamba and Jornada de 12 horas alongside Joel La Leyenda. During 1997, Chamba and Jornada de 12 horas were introduced into a stable known as Thug Life, which consisted of La Leyenda, Hardcastle, Javier Valenzuela (Avitia's childhood friend), Bill Skullion & Rhino Richards. During this period, he won the Unified ICW/MWCW Midwest Tag Team Championship alongside Legend.

He also teamed with Reso (aka Christian Cage) while Copeland was known as Sexton Handcastle, initially being known as Hard Impact but later changing the group's name to The Suicide Blondes. They also worked in Japan as The Canadian Rockers. Avitia once wrestled under the name El MIstico on an episode of WCW Pro, wrestling Meng and James P. Sullivan.

Avitia also worked for the World Wrestling Federation (WWF) in 1996. Canadian wrestler Bret Hart, who saw Copeland wrestle, recommended him to the company and he eventually landed a developmental contract with the WWF in 1997.
